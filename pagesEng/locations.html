Waterfall in Gostilje

In the village of Gostilje, near Zlatibor, there is Gostiljski waterfall which is one of the highest waterfalls in Serbia. Its height is estimated at about 20 m. The environment is rich in beech and pine forests, and medicinal herbs. Like A special feature of this area, the waterfall is one of the most visited tourist destinations destination.

Tornik

Tornik is the highest peak of Zlatibor and is 1496 m high. It's about a dozen away kilometers from Kraljevo Vode, the center of Zlatibor and a famous tourist place. Get to him it comes by going from Voda to Podgorica on the main road and after two kilometers turn right.

Stopića - cave

Stopića cave is one of the most famous caves in Serbia. Located between the villages of Trnava and Rožanstvo, about 250 km from Belgrade, 30 km from Užice and 19 km from regional road Uzice-Adriatic Sea, in the area of Zlatibor. It crosses above the cave road Uzice-Sirogojno.

Ribnica Lake

Only 8 km from the village Zlatibor on the way to the ski center "Tornik" is located Ribnica Lake. The lake is artificial and was created in 1971 when the river was blocked Crni Rzav near Ribnica in order to supply the settlements of Kraljeve vode and Cajetina drinking water. Ribnica Lake is the second largest on Zlatibor.

Museum Old Village Sirogojno

In the village of Sirogojno 26 km from the tourist center Zlatibor, there is the only one open-air museum in Serbia "Staro selo". On a location of five hectares, next to the Church of the Holy Peter and Paul, old log cabins were dragged from all parts of Zlatibor, as it were would be presented and stored here.

Wooden church in Donja Jablanica

Wooden church in Donja Jablanica, a populated place on the territory of the municipality Cajetina, built in 1838, is dedicated to the Protection of the Mother of God. Along with the old one with its cemetery and sobrašice it represents an immovable cultural good as a monument cultures of great importance.
